Build a modern, mobile-first homepage for a web app called “Where My Tax Goes”.

The goal of the homepage is to instantly hook users and drive them into the main interactive feature: “Run the Country”.

STYLE:

* Clean, minimal, and bold
* Large text, high contrast, lots of spacing
* Feels like a mix of Stripe + Apple + a simple game UI
* Smooth animations (fade in, slight movement on scroll)
* Mobile-first design (must look perfect on phone)

HERO SECTION (full screen on load):

* Big headline:
  “See where your tax goes — then run the country yourself”

* Subheading (short, punchy):
  “Explore Australia’s budget in seconds. Adjust taxes, change spending, and build your version of the country.”

* Primary button (large, centered, high contrast):
  “Start Running Australia”

* Secondary subtle link:
  “See the real budget”

* Background idea:
  Soft animated gradient OR blurred budget chart bars moving slowly

---

SECTION 2: “WHERE YOUR TAX GOES”

* Simple visual breakdown (bars or cards):
  Healthcare, Defence, Education, Welfare, Infrastructure

* Each shows:
  % of budget
  AND “$ per person per year”

* Keep it very visual, minimal text

---

SECTION 3: “RUN THE COUNTRY” (main feature preview)

* Title:
  “What would YOU change?”

* Show interactive-style UI preview:

  * Sliders for:
    Healthcare spending
    Defence spending
    Education
    Mining tax
    Corporate tax

* As sliders move (or simulated animation):
  Show:
  “Budget Surplus: +$12B” (or deficit)

* Add line:
  “Balance the budget or build your ideal Australia”

---

SECTION 4: RESULT / IDENTITY PREVIEW

* Show example result card:

  “You are a: Public Services Builder
  You increased mining taxes and boosted healthcare.
  Your Australia runs a $8B surplus.”

* Include mock “Share” button

---

SECTION 5: SOCIAL PROOF / ENGAGEMENT

* “See how others would run Australia”
* Show 2–3 example user budgets (cards)

---

SECTION 6: FINAL CTA

* Big bold section:

  “Ready to build your Australia?”

* Button:
  “Start Running Australia”

---

UX REQUIREMENTS:

* Everything must be extremely simple and fast to understand
* No long paragraphs
* Use icons and visuals over text
* Buttons must be large and thumb-friendly
* Smooth scrolling between sections
* Subtle hover/press animations

---

GOAL:
The user should understand the app in under 3 seconds and feel curious enough to click immediately.

The page should feel slightly playful, slightly serious, and highly shareable.
